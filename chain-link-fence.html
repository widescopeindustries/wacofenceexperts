Chain Link Fence Options

Galvanized Chain Link

Standard galvanized chain link is the most economical option. The zinc coating prevents rust and corrosion for decades. Perfect for backyards, dog runs, and commercial perimeters where appearance is less critical than function.

Best for: Budget-conscious installations, industrial areas, temporary fencing

Vinyl-Coated Chain Link

Color-coated chain link with vinyl covering over galvanized wire. Available in black, green, brown, and white. More attractive than standard galvanized and provides extra protection against the elements.

Best for: Residential properties, tennis courts, areas where appearance matters

Privacy Slats

Add privacy slats to any chain link fence for increased privacy and wind protection. Available in multiple colors to match your property. Easily installed in existing chain link.

Chain Link vs. Other Fencing

vs. Wood: Chain link costs 40-50% less and requires almost no maintenance. Wood looks better but needs staining and repair.

vs. Vinyl: Chain link costs about 60% less than vinyl. Less privacy but unbeatable for security and pet containment.

vs. Wrought Iron: Chain link provides similar security at a fraction of the cost. Easier to install and maintain.

Maintenance Tips

Chain link requires minimal maintenance:

  • Inspect annually for loose posts or damaged fabric
  • Remove debris caught in mesh
  • Check gate hinges and latches
  • Touch up any scratches on vinyl-coated fencing
  • Trim vegetation to prevent rust from moisture

Chain Link Fence FAQ

How long does a chain link fence last in Waco?

Galvanized chain link lasts 20-30+ years in Waco's climate. Vinyl-coated can last even longer with the extra layer of protection. Proper installation with concrete-set posts is key to longevity.

Can I add privacy to a chain link fence?

Yes! Privacy slats slide through the chain link mesh to create 80-90% privacy. Available in multiple colors. You can also use privacy screening fabric or grow vines along the fence.

What gauge chain link should I get?

Residential fences typically use 11-gauge (lighter) or 9-gauge (heavier) wire. For dogs and standard residential use, 11-gauge works great. For commercial or high-security areas, 9-gauge or 6-gauge is better. We'll recommend the right gauge for your needs.

Does chain link fence meet Waco pool codes?

Yes, when properly installed. Waco requires pool fences to be at least 4 feet tall with self-closing, self-latching gates. We ensure all pool fencing installations meet or exceed local safety codes.

Will chain link rust?

Galvanized chain link is coated with zinc to prevent rust. With proper installation and drainage, rust is minimal. Vinyl-coated chain link provides even better rust protection. Any fence will eventually show wear after 20+ years, but chain link holds up remarkably well.

Can chain link fence keep my dog in?

Absolutely! Chain link is one of the best fencing types for dog containment. Make sure the fence height is appropriate for your dog's size and jumping ability. We can also install a bottom rail to prevent digging underneath.
